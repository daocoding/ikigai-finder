# Example — a fast-path walkthrough

A compressed sample run so you can see the shape. Real sessions are longer and more conversational (one question at a time, lots of reflecting back) — this is trimmed to the bones. Persona: **Maya, 34, a burned-out marketing manager.**

> This is illustrative, not a script. Don't read it *to* the user — run *their* journey.

---

**Agent:** Fast (~15 min) or full (~45)? And want to hear the 5 myths first?
**Maya:** Fast. Skip the myths.

### Values → Work Purpose
**Agent:** Who do you respect, and what specifically about them?
**Maya:** My old manager Ana — she made everyone on the team feel like their work mattered. Nobody felt invisible.
*(…4 more questions: childhood move taught her to read rooms; thinks society wastes people; husband says she values "fairness"; would tell a kid "your voice counts," never "just keep your head down.")*
**Agent:** I'm hearing **fairness, being seen, growth, courage**. If you keep three?
**Maya:** Being seen, fairness, growth.
**Agent:** Quick check — "being seen" can depend on others noticing you. Is the deeper thing that *you* want to make others feel seen?
**Maya:** Yes. That's it.
**Agent (Work Purpose):** → *"So people feel their work and their voice actually matter."*
**Maya:** Oof. Yeah.

### Talents (rated ◎/○/△)
*(5 questions)* → **Reading what a person needs before they say it ◎** (strength: deep empathy · shadow: absorbs others' stress) · **Turning messes into clear next steps ○** · **Making quiet people talk ◎** (strength: draws out honesty · shadow: can neglect her own view).

### Love (domains)
*(5 questions)* → **psychology 🔥, team/organizational culture 🔥, writing, education.** ("I read org-psych books for fun. I get angry when good people quit because no one listened.")

### Synthesis
**Step 1 — candidates (Love × Talent), 6 quick hypotheses:**
1. coaching managers to actually hear their teams
2. writing about what makes people feel seen at work
3. designing onboarding so newcomers feel they matter
4. facilitating hard team conversations
5. teaching empathy as a concrete skill
6. researching why good people quit

**Step 2 — filter through her Work Purpose** ("so people feel their work and voice matter"):
> **True Calling:** *"Drawing people out and turning what I hear into clarity, in the domain of workplace culture, so people feel their work and their voice actually matter."*

**Agent:** This is a starting point, not a verdict. Off on values, talent, or love anywhere?
**Maya:** No — that's uncomfortably accurate.

### Means (now job titles are fair game)

| Means | Day-to-day | Why it fits | First step this week | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Team / leadership coach** | 1:1s with managers on listening & team health | Her ◎ empathy + culture domain + purpose, directly | Message 2 manager friends, offer a free practice session | medium |
| **"Culture fix" internal role** | Run retros, onboarding, engagement at her own company | Uses the calling without quitting | Ask her HR lead what owns culture today | short |
| **Newsletter on being-seen-at-work** | Weekly short essay + reader stories | Writing love + her message, builds an audience | Draft & send issue #1 to 10 people | short |
| **Org-psychology certificate** | Evening study, structured | Feeds the psychology 🔥, credentials the pivot | Bookmark 2 programs, compare next weekend | long |

**Daily practices:** in every meeting, ask one question that makes a quiet person speak; jot one note on what someone seemed to *need*.
**Weekly ritual:** Friday, write 150 words on one moment someone felt seen (or didn't).

### Close
**Agent:** Your one step this week?
**Maya:** Draft newsletter issue #1 and send it to 10 people.
**Agent:** That's it. Want me to check in on Friday?

*(Agent fills in `templates/ikigai-result.md` and hands it over.)*

---

Notice the spine: `love × talent = candidates` → `× work purpose = true calling` → `+ the world = means`. The calling has **no job title**; the means are **all** job titles. That separation is the whole trick.
