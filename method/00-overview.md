# 00 — The framework

The whole method in one page. Read this once; it's the map for the five stages.

![The Ikigai model: three pillars overlapping into your true calling, realized through the means out in the world.](../assets/ikigai.svg)

## The claim

"What you want to do" is not out there waiting to be stumbled upon. It is **assembled** from three things you can actually investigate about yourself. Stop searching the outside world for a calling that drops from the sky; instead, *understand yourself* well enough to build it. That's why this is a **self-understanding method**, not a career quiz.

## The three pillars

| Pillar | The question it answers | It becomes the… |
|---|---|---|
| **Values** | *Why* do I work? What state of the world moves me? | **Why** |
| **Talents** | *How* do I naturally operate? What's effortless for me? | **How** |
| **Love** | *What* field pulls my attention? | **What** |

We investigate them in the order **Values → Talents → Love**, because Values produce a **Work Purpose** that we filter everything else through later.

## The two formulas

**Formula 1 — Want-to-do (a hypothesis):**
```
Love (What you love)  ×  Talent (How you're good)  =  a "want-to-do"
```
A domain you love, pursued in a way you're naturally good at. You generate *many* of these. They're cheap hypotheses.

**Formula 2 — True Calling (the real thing):**
```
Want-to-do  ×  Work Purpose (from your Values)  =  your True Calling
```
Run the want-to-dos through your Work Purpose. The one that best *serves that purpose* is your **True Calling**.

## And then — the Means (the part most exercises skip)

```
True Calling  +  the world  =  the Means
```

> Your calling is found **inside** you. The means to realize it are found **outside**, in society.

The calling is *what you do and why* — stated with **no job title**. The **means** are the jobs, roles, projects, and businesses that let you do it. "Helping people heal through deep listening" is a calling; "therapist, hospice volunteer, podcast host, peer-support trainer" are means. Keeping these separate is the single most important move in the whole method — it's why people who "can't find a career they love" actually just never separated the calling from its container.

## Three rules that keep it honest

1. **No job titles in the calling.** Titles are means, not callings. A title can vanish; a calling can be carried into many titles.
2. **The Work Purpose points outward.** It's the impact you want to have on *others*, and it should energize you, not feel like an obligation.
3. **The calling is a hypothesis, not a verdict.** You confirm it by *living* it and noticing the fit — not by feeling certain on day one. (See [misconceptions](misconceptions.md).)
