# Stage 1 — Values → Work Purpose

**Goal:** surface what matters most to this person, rank their top values, and distill a one-sentence **Work Purpose** — the outward impact they want to have on others. This Purpose is the *Why*, and you'll filter the final calling through it in Stage 4.

We do values **first** on purpose: it gives you the lens for everything that follows.

## Run it

1. Ask the values questions for their chosen path (5 fast / 30 full) from [`../questionnaires/fast.md`](../questionnaires/fast.md) or [`../questionnaires/full.md`](../questionnaires/full.md). One at a time. Reflect each answer back briefly.
2. As they talk, **extract value-keywords** — the recurring words and themes ("freedom," "fairness," "not wasting time," "people feeling seen").
3. **Map keywords to the official values** in [`../reference/values-100.md`](../reference/values-100.md). Use the list as a shared vocabulary, not a cage — if their truest word isn't on the list, keep their word. Aim to land on **5–8 candidate values**.
4. **Rank the top 5.** Ask them directly: "If you could only keep three of these, which survive?" Ranking forces clarity.
5. **Derive the Work Purpose** (below).

## Trap values — the most important move in this stage

Some values are **surface wants that hide a deeper value**. If you accept them at face value you'll build a hollow calling. When a top value is one of these, **probe** until you reach what's underneath:

| Trap value | Probe | Often underneath |
|---|---|---|
| **Wealth** | "Money is a tool. What does having it let you *feel* or *do*?" | Freedom, Security, Comfort, Independence |
| **Fame** | "Fame depends on others. If you were famous, what would that let you do?" | Impact, Connection, Expression, Recognition |
| **Popularity** | "What do you get from being liked?" | Belonging, Safety, Connection, Acceptance |
| **Being Loved** | "'Being loved' is passive. What's the active value you want to *create*?" | Connection, Loving, Intimacy, Belonging |
| **Victory** | "Victory is a moment. What do you enjoy *during* the competition?" | Growth, Mastery, Challenge |
| **Achievement** | "After you hit the goal, how do you want to *feel*?" | Contribution, Self-worth, Growth, Purpose |
| **Authority** | "We can't really control others. What does authority let you *exercise*?" | Leadership, Responsibility, Impact, Service |
| **Correctness** | "Needing to be right is often fear of being wrong. Is it Integrity or Truth you want?" | Integrity, Truth, Honesty |

The full set of probes is in [`../reference/values-100.md`](../reference/values-100.md). The pattern: **a value that depends on other people's reactions, or is just a means to something else, is a trap — dig one level down.**

## Deriving the Work Purpose

From their **top 3–5 values**, compose one sentence: *the outward impact they want to have on others through their work.* It answers **"For the sake of what do I work?"**

Rules:
- About impact **on others**, not self-benefit.
- **Energizing**, not obligatory.
- One sentence, under ~20 words.
- **No job titles, no career language.**

| ✓ Good | ✗ Bad |
|---|---|
| "So more people can find work they love." | "To become a successful coach." (self-focused, job title) |
| "So families can feel financially secure and free." | "To achieve my goals." (no outward impact) |
| "So more people live as their real selves." | "To be respected." (depends on others; it's a trap value) |

Offer it to the user as a draft: *"Here's the Work Purpose I'm hearing — does this feel like yours, or is a word off?"* Adjust until they recognize it.

## Before moving on

Summarize: their ranked top values + their Work Purpose, in their language. Get a "yes." Then go to [02-talents.md](02-talents.md).
