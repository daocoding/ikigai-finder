# Stage 3 — Love → the *What*

**Goal:** surface the **fields and domains** that genuinely pull this person's attention. Not activities yet — *fields*. ("Psychology," "architecture," "how communities form," "money and freedom.") You'll combine these with talents in the synthesis.

A "love" here = a domain you're drawn to learn about and think about, **for its own sake** — independent of whether it pays or impresses anyone. The book's tests for it:

- What would you happily **pay money to learn** right now?
- What's actually **on your bookshelf / in your search history**?
- What once made you feel **"this saved me"** or "this is amazing"?
- What **social problem makes you angry** — angry enough to want it fixed? (Anger points at a domain you care about.)
- What do you lose track of time reading or talking about?

## Run it

1. Ask the love questions for their path from [`../questionnaires/fast.md`](../questionnaires/fast.md) / [`../questionnaires/full.md`](../questionnaires/full.md). One at a time; reflect back.
2. Map answers to the domains in [`../reference/interests-100.md`](../reference/interests-100.md) (90 fields, grouped). Again — vocabulary, not cage. If their real love is "the psychology of money" and that's a blend, keep the blend.
3. Gauge **intensity** for each — is this a mild "sure, that's interesting" or a "I can't *not* think about this"? Note the hot ones.
4. Collect **3–6 domains**, with the strongest pulls marked.

## Two cautions

- **Don't let "useful" sneak in.** This stage is about genuine pull, not what they think they *should* be interested in or what's marketable. Marketability is a Stage-5 (Means) concern. If they answer with a "should," gently redirect: "set aside whether it's useful — what actually grabs you?"
- **Mild interest counts.** Per the misconceptions, a love often starts as a faint "huh, that's kind of nice," not a thunderclap. Don't make them wait for a passion that announces itself.

## Reveal & visualize

Show the answer → field mapping (*"'a mentor explained how to survive here' → the domain of helping people through hard transitions"*) and mark intensity. Then render a **domains map**, hottest fields flagged 🔥. Recipe in [visuals.md](visuals.md).

## Before moving on

Summarize their domains, hottest first. Get a "yes, those pull me." Then go to [04-synthesis.md](04-synthesis.md) — where the three pillars finally combine.
