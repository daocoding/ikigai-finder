# Stage 4 — Synthesis → the True Calling

**Goal:** combine the three pillars into a **True Calling**, stated as an activity with **no job title.** This is a two-step move. **Do not collapse it into one** — the two steps are what make the result both expansive *and* aligned.

You arrive here holding:
- **Love** → domains that pull them (Stage 3)
- **Talents** → how they naturally work, ◎ first (Stage 2)
- **Work Purpose** → the outward impact they want (Stage 1)

## Step 1 — Generate candidates (Love × Talent)

```
Love (a domain)  ×  Talent (a way of working)  =  a "want-to-do" candidate
```

Combine their domains and talents into **6–8 diverse candidate activities.** This is a brainstorm: **quantity over quality.** Vary the pairings — pair domain A with talent 1, then domain A with talent 2, then domain B with talent 1, etc. Lead with ◎ talents and hottest domains, but don't only use those — surprising pairs are often the good ones.

Each candidate **must be a concrete verb phrase**, never a role:
- ✓ "Breaking down how money really works so anxious people feel calm"
- ✓ "Designing physical spaces that make strangers talk to each other"
- ✗ "Financial advisor" · ✗ "Architect" (those are means, and we're not there yet)

Show the user the list. Let them react — cross some off, star the ones that spark.

## Step 2 — Filter by Work Purpose → the True Calling

```
candidates  ×  Work Purpose (from your Values)  =  your True Calling
```

Now pass the candidates through their **Work Purpose** from Stage 1. Ask: **which of these, if you did it, would most create the impact you said you care about?** The candidate (occasionally two) that best *serves the purpose* is the **True Calling.**

Compose the statement:

> **[how they naturally work] in [the domain they love], so that [their work purpose].**

Examples:
- "Listening deeply and asking the question that unlocks people, in the domain of personal finance, so that families can feel secure and free."
- "Making the complex simple and systematic, in the domain of psychology, so more people understand and accept themselves."

Keep it under ~30 words. **Still no job title.**

## Reveal the logic (diagram it)

This is the step users most distrust as a black box — so **diagram the formula itself**, don't just announce a result. Show the Love × Talent pairings becoming candidates, then the Work-Purpose filter **keeping some and dropping others, with the reason on the dropped ones** — what you *rejected* is half the proof that the filter is real. The flowchart recipe is in [visuals.md](visuals.md). Laying it out is exactly what lets the user say "wait, don't drop that one" or "C2 matters more than C1" — that pushback *is* the audit working, and it's how the calling ends up genuinely theirs.

## Present it as a hypothesis, not a verdict

This matters as much as the content. Say, in your own words:

> "This is a **starting point, not a final answer.** You confirm it by *living* it for a while and noticing the fit — not by feeling 100% certain today."

Then offer the **3-axis check** so they can feel whether it's right:
1. **Off on values?** Does any part feel like it betrays what you said matters most?
2. **Off on talent?** Does it lean on something that drains you rather than energizes you?
3. **Off on love?** Is the domain one you'd actually choose to spend years in?

If any axis feels off, **go back and adjust** — re-pick a candidate, swap a talent, re-rank a value. Iterate *with* them until they read the statement and quietly recognize themselves in it.

## Before moving on

Lock the True Calling statement (their words matter more than yours). Capture the **method**, the **domain**, and the **work purpose** separately too — Stage 5 needs all three. Then go to [05-means.md](05-means.md) to bring it into the world.
