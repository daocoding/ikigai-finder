# The 5 misconceptions (drop these before you start)

From Chapter 1 of the book. Most people are stuck not because they lack a calling but because they're holding one of these myths. Share them with the user before the journey — naming them out loud dissolves a surprising amount of the block.

| ✗ The myth | ✓ The truth |
|---|---|
| It must be something you'll do **for your whole life**. | Just do **what you most want to do *now***. It can change. |
| When you find it, you'll feel **"this is destiny."** | At first it's usually only **mild interest** — a faint "huh, that's kind of nice." |
| It must be something that **benefits other people**. | **Living for yourself also helps others.** Start from your own genuine wanting. |
| You have to **act more / try more things** to find it. | **Knowing yourself** is how you find it. Action without self-understanding just adds noise. |
| What you want to do **can't become your work.** | The **"what" is inside you; the means are out in the world.** They connect — that's the whole second half of this journey. |

> Read the last row twice. It's the thesis of the entire method, and the reason this journey has a **Means** stage and not just a "calling" stage.
