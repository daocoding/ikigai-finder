# Full questionnaire (~45–60 min)

30 questions per pillar (90 total) + an 8-angle deep dive on the strongest talent. Ask **one at a time**, in the user's language (these are written in English; run the conversation in whatever language the user uses). You don't have to ask all 30 mechanically — read the room: if a few rich answers have already surfaced clear patterns, move faster through the rest or let the user skip ones that don't land. Depth on a few answers beats thin answers to all.

Order: Values → Talents (→ 8-angle deep dive) → Love. Method for each stage is in [`../method/`](../method/).

---

## 1. Values — 30 questions
→ then derive the **Work Purpose**. See [`../method/01-values.md`](../method/01-values.md).

1. Who do you respect? What specifically about them?
2. Who has influenced you the most in life?
3. What do you like / dislike about your father's lifestyle?
4. What do you like / dislike about your mother's lifestyle?
5. What do you want people to say about you at your funeral?
6. What is your favorite book, and why?
7. What moves you to tears?
8. At 80 years old, what would you regret *not* having done?
9. Who do you respect the *least*? Why?
10. What made you happiest in elementary school?
11. What was a key turning-point decision in your life? What criteria did you use?
12. What are you most proud of?
13. Who is your best friend? What do you like about them?
14. When have you worked the hardest?
15. What brands do you like?
16. List everything you're interested in.
17. What is unforgivable to you?
18. When do you feel happiest?
19. If anything you wrote down would come true in 5 years, what would you write?
20. What factors did you weigh in your biggest life decisions?
21. What makes you feel proud at work?
22. What impact do you want to have on the people around you?
23. Is your current use of time meaningful to you?
24. Who do you respect in your workplace?
25. What do you help other people with?
26. Who was your best boss?
27. Who was your worst boss?
28. What do you want to eliminate from your life (e.g. lying, commuting)?
29. What makes you want to swear in anger?
30. List 10 things you wish were different in the world.

## 2. Talents — 30 questions
→ rate each ◎ / ○ / △ (defined in the method file), then run the 8-angle deep dive on the strongest. See [`../method/02-talents.md`](../method/02-talents.md).

1. What have you been good at since childhood?
2. What can you do effortlessly that others find hard?
3. When were you so absorbed you forgot the time?
4. What do people thank you for?
5. Ask a friend: "What are my weaknesses?" — then flip each one to find the strength behind it.
6. What was your biggest failure? (Where did you waste effort?)
7. What do you like about yourself?
8. What tasks do you find annoying? (Flip them.)
9. What do you feel is missing in yourself?
10. What have you done naturally, without getting tired?
11. What makes you think "why can't other people do this?"
12. What have you been praised for?
13. What's your personality, according to others?
14. What have you struggled with for a long time?
15. What work could you do forever?
16. When do you feel excited?
17. What do you do on your days off?
18. What did teachers / parents scold you for? (Often a hidden talent.)
19. What job would you absolutely hate?
20. What can you do for hours without fatigue?
21. When do you feel most "you"?
22. Describe a recent happy day.
23. What "vibe" do you give off?
24. What are you praised for without even trying?
25. What do you do unconsciously?
26. What is your source of motivation?
27. What do you do faster than other people?
28. When do you feel most efficient?
29. What activity feels "comfortable"?
30. List 10 things you don't mind doing.

### 8-angle deep dive (talents — deep)
Pick the user's **single strongest talent** (a ◎) and a specific time they felt fulfilled using it. Walk these 8 angles. Output: the *reusable mechanism* behind the talent.

1. What did you do *before* you felt that fulfillment?
2. What were the characteristics of the environment?
3. What specific actions did you take?
4. What was the thinking behind those actions?
5. What were you aware of / noticing at the time?
6. What was your motivation?
7. When did the fulfillment fade — and how could you keep it?
8. Looking back, what could you have done better?

## 3. Love — 30 questions
→ map to domains. See [`../method/03-love.md`](../method/03-love.md).

1. What would you pay to study?
2. What topics excite you?
3. What makes you happy?
4. If money weren't an issue, what work would you do?
5. If all jobs had equal status, what would you do?
6. What have you enjoyed learning?
7. What do you want to study right now?
8. What did you dream of being in elementary school?
9. What games were you obsessed with?
10. What new thing do you want to try?
11. What is your favorite book?
12. What worries have you overcome?
13. What social issues bother you?
14. What do you spend the most time on?
15. What do you talk about with friends?
16. What would you do with a one-week holiday?
17. What do you spend money on that others think is expensive?
18. What do you regret not having done yet?
19. List your hobbies, from childhood to now.
20. What do you search for online?
21. What was your favorite subject?
22. What TV shows do you like?
23. What "saved" you in the past?
24. Ask friends: "What do I look interested in?"
25. What makes you ask "why?" or "how?"
26. What themes have interested you for years?
27. Which field best fits your work purpose?
28. What activities can you *not* enjoy? (The contrast is informative.)
29. Look at yourself from outside: what does this person like?
30. Who do you follow on social media?
